Key findings

What the data reveals

Combining four independent data sources, the analysis confirms that climate vulnerability in Athens is not random — it follows socioeconomic fault lines with statistical precision.

+0.53°C
The Luxury Effect confirmed
Municipalities with unemployment above the median are statistically hotter than affluent areas. High unemployment correlates with low NDVI and high LST across all five years.
−54%
Vegetation gap
NDVI ranges from 0.16 in Agia Varvara to 0.45 in Acharnon. Wealthier peripheral municipalities consistently show higher vegetation density and lower surface temperatures.
2023
Peak flood year
691 water pumping operations in 2023 alone — a 55% increase over 2020. Athens municipality accounts for the largest share, exposing a disproportionate flood burden on dense urban cores.

Affluent areas stay cool.
Deprived areas overheat.

Methodology

How it was built

A 6-script Python pipeline extracts, cleans, merges and scores data from four independent sources into a single master dataset of 330 observations.

Satellite data extraction 01_extract_satellite_data.py
Landsat 8/9 Collection 2 median composites per summer (Jun–Aug) 2020–2024. Zonal statistics per municipality using GADM Level 3 boundaries. LST, NDVI, NDBI extracted at 100m resolution via Google Earth Engine.
Flood risk extraction 02_extract_flood_data.py
Fire Service urban intervention records filtered for "ΑΝΤΛΗΣΗ ΝΕΡΟΥ" (water pumping) events across Attica 2020–2024. 2,514 flood-related incidents aggregated per municipality and year.
Socioeconomic data 03_extract_socioeconomic_data.py
ELSTAT Census 2021 — unemployment rate per municipality (Table B02). Used as proxy for socioeconomic vulnerability, normalized 0–1 for the composite index.
Vulnerability Index 05_vulnerability_index.py
Environmental Vulnerability Index computed as equal-weight composite: 25% LST + 25% (1−NDVI) + 25% flood risk + 25% unemployment. Methodology based on Cutter, Boruff & Shirley (2003).
EVI = 0.25 × LSTnorm + 0.25 × (1 − NDVInorm) + 0.25 × floodnorm + 0.25 × unemploymentnorm
Cutter, S.L., Boruff, B.J. & Shirley, W.L. (2003). Social Vulnerability to Environmental Hazards. Social Science Quarterly, 84(2), 242–261.
