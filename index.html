Four independent data sources, one clear pattern: climate vulnerability in Athens follows socioeconomic fault lines with statistical precision.

The Luxury Effect

Affluent areas stay cool.
Deprived areas overheat.

Municipalities were split at the median unemployment rate (12.1%). The temperature differential is statistically consistent across all five years.

Affluent municipalities · unemployment ≤ 12.1%
44.37°C
Higher tree coverage, more parks, lower building density. The combination of vegetation and reduced impervious surfaces creates a measurable cooling effect — confirmed by five consecutive summers of Landsat imagery.
Deprived municipalities · unemployment > 12.1%
44.90°C
Dense impervious surfaces, minimal vegetation, intensified urban heat island effect. Residents in these areas face compounded vulnerability: higher temperatures, fewer green spaces, and greater flood exposure.
+0.53°C
Statistically consistent temperature gap between deprived and affluent municipalities — confirmed by satellite data across five consecutive summers (2020–2024). Methodology: Cutter, Boruff & Shirley (2003).
Methodology

How it was built

A 6-script Python pipeline extracts, cleans, merges and scores data from four independent sources into a single master dataset of 330 observations.

01 · 01_extract_satellite_data.py
Satellite extraction
Landsat 8/9 Collection 2 median composites per summer (Jun–Aug) 2020–2024. Zonal statistics per municipality using GADM Level 3 boundaries. LST, NDVI, NDBI extracted at 100m resolution via Google Earth Engine.
02 · 02_extract_flood_data.py
Flood risk extraction
Fire Service urban intervention records filtered for "ΑΝΤΛΗΣΗ ΝΕΡΟΥ" (water pumping) events across Attica 2020–2024. 2,514 flood-related incidents aggregated per municipality and year.
03 · 03_extract_socioeconomic_data.py
Socioeconomic data
ELSTAT Census 2021 — unemployment rate per municipality (Table B02). Used as proxy for socioeconomic vulnerability, normalized 0–1 for the composite index.
04 · 04_merge_master_dataset.py
Master dataset merge
All sources merged on normalized municipality key. 92% Landsat match rate across 66 municipalities. Remaining 8% are islands outside the GADM bounding box — expected and documented.
05 · 05_vulnerability_index.py
Vulnerability Index (EVI)
Environmental Vulnerability Index computed as equal-weight composite. Each component normalized 0–1. EVI categorized into five levels from Very Low to Very High. Methodology based on Cutter et al. (2003).
06 · 06_prepare_powerbi_data.py
PowerBI preparation
Final cleaning and formatting for PowerBI import. Greek municipality names added for labelling. Three output files: time series, 2024 snapshot, and master dataset.
EVI  =  0.25 × LSTnorm  +  0.25 × (1 − NDVInorm)  +  0.25 × floodnorm  +  0.25 × unemploymentnorm
Cutter, S.L., Boruff, B.J. & Shirley, W.L. (2003). Social Vulnerability to Environmental Hazards. Social Science Quarterly, 84(2), 242–261.
